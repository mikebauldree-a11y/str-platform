🌴 Market Overview

Aruba is one of the most reliable and investor‑friendly STR markets in the Caribbean. With year‑round warm weather, minimal hurricane exposure, and a tourism economy built around high‑spending visitors, Aruba consistently delivers strong occupancy and ADR performance.

The island attracts travelers from the U.S., Canada, and Europe, with repeat visitation rates among the highest in the Caribbean. Unlike many island markets with sharp seasonal swings, Aruba maintains steady demand across all 12 months thanks to its dry climate, strong airlift, and well‑developed hospitality infrastructure.

STR supply is growing but still constrained by limited land availability and strict development controls. This creates a favorable environment for investors who can offer well‑located condos, villas, and boutique units near Palm Beach, Eagle Beach, Noord, and Oranjestad.

📈 Seasonality & Demand Patterns

Aruba’s seasonality curve is one of the smoothest in the Caribbean.

  • High Season: December – April
  • Shoulder Season: May – August
  • Low Season: September – October (still stronger than most islands)

Occupancy often remains above 65–70% even in low season, with high season pushing 85–90%+ for well‑positioned properties.

🏛 Local STR Regulations

Aruba is considered one of the more STR‑friendly islands, but regulations vary by zoning and property type.

  • STRs are allowed in most tourist zones (Palm Beach, Eagle Beach, Noord).
  • Some condo associations restrict short‑term rentals — always verify HOA rules.
  • Tourist tax and environmental levies apply to STR stays.
  • No island‑wide ban or cap on STRs as of 2026.
